# ALASKA.AI Dispatch — 2026-06-26

## Story
A physics-informed AI **digital twin** watches permafrost thaw under a road embankment in
**Utqiagvik, Alaska**, fusing a physics heat-transfer model with machine learning and a buried
fiber-optic sensor array, and forecasts where the frozen ground is going.

- **AI angle:** a digital twin (physics + machine learning) re-calibrated by two ~1 km fiber-optic
  cables (thermal + seismic), forecasting permafrost thaw under infrastructure.
- **Honest caveat (editor's framing, not attributed to the researchers):** the twin forecasts the
  thaw but cannot drill the ground to prove it, and cannot say which mile of road fails first. A
  model predicts; the ground is the proof.
