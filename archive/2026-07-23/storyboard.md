# Dispatch storyboard — Counting Belugas From Orbit (2026-07-23)

**Angle (curious, wonder-forward):** NOAA is teaching a computer to find Alaska's rarest
whales from space, a genuinely new way to see the nearly unseeable. It cannot count belugas
yet, and whether it ever can turns on one very Alaskan question, can it get a clear look
through a slice of sky the Port of Anchorage and Elmendorf already fill.

**Hero:** SatelliteEye (net-new library asset). **Subject:** the Cook Inlet beluga, treated
with care. **Palette:** pewter-teal glacial silt, orbital blue-black, detection cyan to
warning amber (stakes and claims only), beluga bone-white.

## Shots
1. **THE SILT / FIND THE WHALE** (0.0-7.4s) top-down silt water, a pale beluga smudge
   resolving under a cyan reticle. The squint-scroll-stop.
2. **THE STAKES** (7.4-15.5s) 331 left (2022 aerial survey, NOT the AI), down ~80% since
   1979, a plunging curve with a faint hopeful uptick.
3. **FROM SPACE** (15.5-21.4s) the SatelliteEye rises to orbit, imaging cone reaching down.
4. **GAIA + THE PIPELINE** (21.4-33.2s) name + partners (NOAA, USGS, Microsoft, Navy), then
   the moving cutaway: EarthExplorer retrieval, expert human annotation, the ML detector
   learning one labeled whale at a time.
5. **STILL LEARNING** (33.2-42.7s) the satellite strains, cannot count belugas yet, needs a
   clear look straight down.
6. **THE TURN, CROWDED SKY** (42.7-54.6s) airspace rings (Port of Anchorage, JBER) crowd the
   imaging cone, LIKELY BOOKED, and June 2025 came back with no imagery. The detection box
   shows a question mark, not a score.
7. **THE BUTTON / LOOP** (54.6-67.3s) back to the silt and the pale whale, the eye softening
   to a patient blink. The whales may be holding on, the eye is real. The question is
   whether the sky it needs ever stays open.

## Honesty guardrails held
- 331 is an aerial-survey number, never attributed to the AI.
- The AI is experimental, building training data, not a deployed counter (CANNOT COUNT YET).
- The June 2025 cause is NOAA's LIKELY, shown as correlation not stated cause.
- Amber never lands a confirmed find on the whale; the eye stays cyan all run.
- The beluga is never a comic character.
