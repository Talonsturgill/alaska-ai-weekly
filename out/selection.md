## Mode
**Deep Dive.** One structural tension dominates: the Air Force published specific parcel addresses for data centers near Eielson AFB and JBER, with private bids due June 29 — before the Alaska Senate votes on the LNG pipeline tax bill (June 19) that would provide the gas to power them. The lease solicitation is sequenced backwards: bids first, power later, community input never.

## Lead
**Air Force RFLP AFCEC-26-R-0006 — June 5 formal release, June 29 bid deadline, community pushback.**

The Department of the Air Force formally issued its Request for Lease Proposal on June 5, offering 12 specific parcels across 4,700 acres at JBER, Eielson AFB, and Clear Space Force Station for commercial AI data center development. Proposals from private developers are due June 29. North Pole and Fairbanks-area residents are raising concerns about noise, permafrost degradation, water use, air quality from diesel backup generators, and proximity to schools — some proposed Eielson-adjacent parcels sit less than a mile from North Pole Middle School. SAM.gov solicitation AFCEC-26-R-0006, filed by contracting officer Julia A. Cantrell, is the primary source. KTUU reported June 9 (Alex Bengel). Fairbanks News-Miner reported June 11 (Jack Barnwell).

The power foundation: The same week, the Alaska House passed HB 381 on June 12 by a 34-5 vote — an 80-90% reduction in property tax on the proposed Glenfarne LNG pipeline — and sent it to a Senate with "substantially more skepticism." The special session expires June 19. Glenfarne president Adam Prestidge announced June 8 a statutory cap of $16/MMBtu on North Slope gas prices to Cook Inlet utilities, framed as protection against projected $17+ imported LNG. But the pipeline itself is still years away even if HB 381 passes, and the Air Force's own March 2026 site analysis flagged JBER parcels as having "insufficient power" requiring new Chugach connections. GVEA cautioned that major loads must connect to its 138-kV line — capacity that's already constrained.

**The sequence is backwards.** Bids close June 29. The Senate energy vote is June 19. Private companies are being asked to price 20-year leases on parcels for which no committed power supply exists.

## Supporting Story 1: UAF ML Bycatch Model
**UAF graduate student Marcel Gietzmann-Sanders published a peer-reviewed ML model in Animal Biotelemetry (June 8 UAF press release, multiple outlets June 9-10).** The model uses 700,000+ data points from a 13-year Chinook salmon tagging program to predict where salmon sit in the water column, giving pollock trawlers actionable guidance to avoid bycatch. Navy-funded. Direct economic benefit: bycatch overages shut down the pollock fishery in 2024. Primary source (UAF PR + Springer journal).

This is the Vol. 01 "third option" made concrete: domain-specific AI, tens of kilowatts not hundreds of megawatts, expertise the rest of the country doesn't have. It belongs as a one-paragraph supporting beat showing what productive Alaska AI looks like.

## Supporting Story 2: $272M RHTP — Tribal Health Orgs Leading Tech Proposals
**ADN June 7 (Iris Samuels) and News-Miner June 9 (Jack Barnwell):** Alaska's Department of Health advanced 400+ of ~1,800 RHTP proposals. Tanana Chiefs Conference drone vaccine delivery to 27 Interior villages is among the advancers. Rep. Genevieve Mina noted that federal restrictions on construction spending "almost direct" applicants toward AI-enabled care. 22% of proposals are "data and technology" category.

This supports the lead by showing that the rural/tribal health AI buildout is happening at a different scale and cadence than the military hyperscale leases — with tribal organizations driving it, not waiting for federal landlord decisions.
