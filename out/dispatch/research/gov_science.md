# Gov/academic science beat findings (2026-07-02 run)

1. **UAF Chinook ML depth model for trawler bycatch avoidance** — STRONG. Marcel Gietzmann-Sanders (UAF grad student, College of Fisheries and Ocean Sciences, w/ Andrew Seitz) applied ML to 700,000+ data points from 13 years of satellite-tagged Chinook (depth/time/temp/light every 5-10s) + Copernicus Marine environmental layers -> depth-distribution prediction model for Gulf of Alaska + Bering Sea. Published in Animal Biotelemetry (DOI 10.1186/s40317-026-00456-2). Gives pollock trawlers depth/timing guidance to avoid Chinook bycatch; in 2024 two boats' weekend bycatch closed the pollock fishery. Tagging primarily funded by US Navy. Quote: "It shows what you can find out when you collect enough data about a species over a long enough time." (Gietzmann-Sanders). Sources: UAF News 06-08 (primary), Alaska Native News 06-09, Juneau Empire 06-10, Homer News/Peninsula Clarion 06-10, Springer paper. Caveats: algorithm architecture not named; paper paywalled; model is prediction of typical depth patterns, not real-time fish location.
