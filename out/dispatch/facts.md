# ALASKA.AI Dispatch 2026-07-15 — VERIFIED FACT PACK

## Story (LOCKED, cleared adversarial fact-check gate)
A Penn State-led team built a permafrost "digital twin" for a road embankment in
Utqiagvik, Alaska. It pairs physics-based heat-transfer modeling with machine learning
that continuously updates from buried fiber-optic sensor data, forecasting how the frozen
ground beneath Arctic infrastructure warms, thaws, and moves heat.

## Verified facts (primary-sourced; use only these on screen)
- Site: a road embankment in Utqiagvik, Alaska (northernmost U.S. city), near Elson Lagoon.
- Sensors: TWO buried fiber-optic cables, each 1 kilometer (0.6 mile) long, reading thermal
  and seismic data (distributed fiber-optic sensing).
- Data window: September 2021 to June 2024 (about three years).
- Method: a "digital twin" = a physics model (equations + AI-powered machine learning) that
  forecasts heat transfer through the ground, plus a second model that ingests the real-time
  fiber-optic data and continuously UPDATES the forecast's parameters (e.g., how fast heat
  moves through the soil).
- Published June 16, 2026 in Journal of Geophysical Research: Earth Surface,
  DOI 10.1029/2025JF008787. Also featured in Eos.
- People/orgs: Ming Xiao (Penn State civil engineering, corresponding author); Tieyuan Zhu
  (Penn State geosciences); Eileen Martin (Colorado School of Mines); Xiaohang Ji (AECOM);
  additional collaborators include UAF (Dmitry Nicolsky), Ohio State, UW-Madison.

## The honest AI angle
A digital twin: a live, self-correcting mirror of the frozen ground. It watches the real
sensors and forecasts the thaw a step ahead, so engineers can protect roads, runways, and
pipes before the ground gives way.

## The ONE honest caveat (draw it, do not leave it to a text card)
It forecasts the thaw, it does not stop it. And it is proven at ONE embankment on about three
years of data, not across the Arctic. A twin can predict the ground; it cannot freeze it back.
(Xiao himself notes a decade more of data "would be extremely valuable.")
