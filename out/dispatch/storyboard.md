# Storyboard — Dispatch 2026-07-01 — "Yakutat: the machine drew the hidden edge" (rev. 2, post-critic)

**Concept.** A machine-learning model combed years of Alaska seismic recordings and **ignited about 1,750**
uncatalogued earthquakes out of the noise under its scan. They were not scattered — they fell into one
**250-kilometer line**, the **razor edge** of the **Yakutat microplate** grinding under Alaska. We watch the
edge appear under the scan, become a line, become a diving slab, loom toward the **Denali Fault** that broke
in 2002 (**M7.9**), and then hit its honest limit. The machine drew the hidden edge.

**Honest caveat (the thesis, DRAWN not captioned).** The model shows *where* the edge sits. It cannot tell
you *when* it moves. Staged as: the crimson edge locks razor-sharp in space (WHERE), while a time axis stays
fogged and refuses to resolve (WHEN). The Denali link is a hypothesis the researchers **propose** — rendered
as a ghosted/dotted vector, never a confident causal arrow.

**Style mode:** editorial data-tectonics / seismic reveal. **Dials:** design 7 · motion 6 · density 6 ·
color 7 · grain 6 · grade 6.
