# DISPATCH 2026-07-14 — THE CLAIM ON THE TUNDRA

**Story:** Stak Energy has applied to lease a square mile of North Slope state land about 25 miles
south of Deadhorse to build a gigawatt-scale, gas-fired data center for AI and cloud compute. The
public comment record is lopsided (more than 500 comments, fewer than a dozen in favor) and the
state's decision deadline is July 17th, 2026. Fact-check verdict: CLEAR_WITH_LABELS.

**Honest AI angle + caveat (drawn, not stated):** the AI-compute boom needs land and power, and it
is asking Alaska for both. The real limit is that this is a *proposal*, not a data center: nothing
is built, the cost figures span six times over (~$500M initial site work in the lease documents vs
the company's own "over $10 billion" full-build estimate), the power is at least a gigawatt (~30%
above all of urban Alaska's peak demand), and there are no confirmed customers or chips. The check
is the public process. Pro-Alaska framing: the land and the decision belong to Alaskans, closing
July 17th. No savior, no foregone-victim.
