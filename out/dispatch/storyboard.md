# Dispatch storyboard — 2026-07-10

## Concept
**Teaching the Machine to See.** To retire a 70-year hand count of Bristol Bay sockeye, a person still
has to mark the fish one at a time so a drone's model can learn to watch. And until it is tested against
the Fish and Game towers, the towers keep the count.

Story: a summer pilot on the **Wood River** (Bristol Bay) is training a computer-vision model on
automated drone footage to count sockeye escapement, the first attempt to augment a manual counting-tower
tradition. Sources: KDLG (2026-07-03), Alaska Public Media mirror (2026-07-08).

## The one honest caveat (drawn, not captioned)
The drone **cannot count yet**. A person must first hand-label **several thousand** fish to teach it; it
has **not been tested against the tower counts** (that is the next phase); and a hard case like a **shadow
on the river** may still need much more training. Fish and Game runs the towers but is **not** a partner in
the pilot. We draw the caveat: a sockeye slipping through a shadow band the model misses, stamped NOT YET
TESTED.
