Post text (copy/paste)

A Fairbanks power co-op just paid $50,000 for the right to wait in line.

Golden Valley Electric Association wants a second turbine at its North Pole plant, and the line it is standing in is a global one. GE Vernova is sitting on a turbine backlog near 100 gigawatts, about a fifth of it tied to data centers. Siemens Energy says data centers now make up more than 60 percent of this year's gas turbine orders. Neither company says "AI." The industry press does, and the arithmetic is not subtle: this is the AI buildout eating the world's supply of firm power equipment, and a small Interior Alaska utility is now bidding against it.

GVEA has a real reason to want the megawatts. It lost cheap purchased power from Southcentral in 2024, its Enstar gas contract ended in 2025, and it burns up to 250,000 gallons of diesel a day some winters. So the co-op paid a $50,000 deposit in April just to hold a manufacturing slot, ahead of a board vote on July 28 for a second gas turbine priced between 90 and 120 million dollars, plus a separate bet on battery storage. Read one way, that is a utility that saw a supply squeeze coming and moved early. That is a genuinely underrated skill in 2026.

Read the other way: North Pole already fails the federal PM2.5 standard, and a member and clean-air advocate, Patrice Lee, has raised real concern about adding a combustion turbine in a place that already fails the air test. A 36 megawatt wind project in Delta Junction has been floated as an alternative. Nobody has shown, in public, that wind and batteries could cover Fairbanks on the coldest night of the year. Nobody has shown they could not, either.

The real story is not "AI is coming for Alaska's power." It is that a slow moving global hardware market now sets deadlines for small utility boards, and the tradeoff those boards face, cost versus air versus certainty, has to get made in public, on a clock they did not set.

If you sat on that board, would you sign for the turbine on July 28, or bet the winter on wind and batteries that are not built yet?
