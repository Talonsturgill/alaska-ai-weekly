**AI drones vs the 70-year hand-count: a pilot is training a computer-vision model on
automated drone imagery to count Bristol Bay sockeye escapement, tested this 2026 season
against Alaska Department of Fish and Game counting towers used since 1955.**

Anchor sources: KDLG (Dillingham public radio) 2026-07-03; Alaska Public Media / KMXT

## Why this story (selection criteria in order)
1. **Concrete Alaska impact**: escapement counts govern in-season management of the
   world's largest sockeye fishery, the economic backbone of Bristol Bay's predominantly
   Alaska Native communities. A more accurate/automated count changes when the fleet can fish.
2. **Visual potential**: exceptionally high. Old vs new (a human hand-clicker at a 1955-era
   tower counting in 10-minute intervals vs an autonomous drone box launching at 8:30 a.m.);
   a braided river + tower network; escapement quantity as a countable field; a 1955->2026
   timeline; computer-vision bounding boxes on schooling sockeye.
3. **Tangibility**: a real, on-the-ground 2026 deployment (not a proposal), during the peak run.
4. **"Send it to a coworker?"**: yes. Fisheries + AI + a trust question ("would you believe an
   AI number that decides when you can fish?") is highly shareable in-state, statewide pickup already.
