# Selection - 2026-07-22 - Carousel No. 14

## SELECTED: The state's free land for data centers (AIDEA Houston / Mat-Su)
story_id: `aidea-houston-park`

**One-line thesis:** Alaska has 8 data centers. Rather than wait for more, the
state just moved to give away roughly 31 square miles of public land, for free,
to its own development authority to manufacture the conditions for them, and a
town of 2,000 next door is trying to ban them. You can comment until Aug 19.

### Why this story (criteria in order)
1. **Strongest concrete Alaska impact + cleanest AI frame.** Data centers are
   the physical infrastructure of AI. Of the three surviving in-window stories,
   this is the only one squarely on the Alaska AI Docket's mandate (AI
   infrastructure decisions in Alaska). The runner-up (NDAA) is "autonomy" and
   "analytics/simulation" money the fact-checker explicitly warned must NOT be
   relabeled AI, and only $10M of it is Alaska-tied. This story is a real state
   land decision with a live public process Alaskans can act on now.
2. **Visual potential.** A township-scale parcel (19,950 acres) stamped on the
   Mat-Su against a familiar reference; the 8-vs-639 comparison; a decision
   timeline with a live Aug 19 gate; the four-rooms access read (this is an
   OPEN room). Land + scale + civic-process geometry, not a power-bill line
   chart (which 07-18 already did).
3. **Tangibility.** Free public land, a named parcel two miles north of Houston,
   a named council member (Lisa Johansen) trying to ban it, a real deadline.
4. **Send to a coworker?** Yes. It is local, it is a giveaway of a public asset,
   and it is actionable. High civic salience (scout F: heavy community signal).

### Verified spine (from claims.json, all verified)
- 19,950 acres (~31 sq mi), ADL 234762, DNR preliminary decision 2026-07-10.
- Noncompetitive, NO-COST conveyance to AIDEA; ~2 mi north of Houston, Mat-Su.
- Public comment through 5 p.m. Wed Aug 19, 2026.
- Planned uses include "scalable data centers" (per decision + coverage).
- Alaska has 8 data centers (among the fewest of any state); Virginia has 639.
- Houston council member Lisa Johansen has proposed a city data-center ban.

  the decision attachment and local coverage. Attribute it, do not put it in the
  state's mouth as the notice text.
- "60 road miles north of Anchorage" and "41 pages" were DROPPED (unverifiable).
  Do not use either on a slide.
- "8 data centers / 639 in Virginia" is reported by one outlet (Alaska's News
  Source). Frame as reported, and keep it as comparison scale, not a precise
  federal census.
