Alaska is moving to hand 31 square miles of public land to its own development agency for zero dollars.

The proposed conveyance covers 19,950 acres two miles north of Houston, planned as an industrial and energy district that would host data centers. The land itself is the subsidy, offered before a single tenant has to show up.

The deck walks through the deed, the data-center gap the state is chasing, the town council member fighting it, and the comment window that closes at 5 p.m. on August 19.

Is free public land a fair way to attract an industry, or should the state set a price the neighbors help decide?
